All-Sky Imaging Observations at Maitri and Bharati Stations, Antarctica

Continuous All-Sky Imaging (ASI) observations are being conducted at the Maitri and Bharati research stations in Antarctica, operated by the National Centre for Polar and Ocean Research (NCPOR). These observations form a critical component of ongoing atmospheric and space weather research programs, with a specific focus on high-latitude geophysical processes, particularly auroral dynamics and magnetosphere–ionosphere coupling.

The collected datasets facilitate detailed investigations into the onset and evolution of auroral substorms, and provide key insights into the physical mechanisms governing the transport of energy and charged particles from the magnetosphere into the polar ionosphere and thermosphere. These interactions play a significant role in space weather variability and have direct implications for ionospheric conductivity, particle precipitation, and electromagnetic energy dissipation at high latitudes.

Scientific Objectives

  • Monitoring Auroral Activity To characterize the spatial and temporal variability of auroral activity in response to solar wind–magnetosphere interactions, and to quantify their impact on ionospheric electrodynamics and upper atmospheric energy balance.

  • Substorm Onset and Magnetospheric Coupling To investigate the trigger mechanisms and morphological features of auroral substorm onset, and to examine the associated magnetospheric energy transfer processes, including field-aligned currents, particle precipitation, and auroral arc dynamics in the polar ionosphere.

Instrument Details

  • Type: All Sky Imaging System
  • Field of View: 180° (fisheye lens)
  • Resolution: Up to 4K (varies by deployment)
  • Image Interval: Every 1 minute (configurable)
  • Deployed at: Maitri and Bharati Station, Antarctica
